Publishing Hygiene

Cybersecurity professionals audit before a breach. Publishing should work the same way.

Artists need metadata hygiene, ownership hygiene, registration hygiene, and agreement hygiene. Most have none of them. Here's where to start.

Ownership Hygiene

  • Copyright registrations filed with USCO
  • Chain of title documentation on file
  • No unresolved ownership disputes
  • Work-for-hire agreements documented where applicable

Metadata Hygiene

  • ISRC codes assigned to every recording
  • ISWC codes registered for compositions
  • Artist name consistent across all platforms
  • Distributor metadata matches PRO registration

Registration Hygiene

  • All works registered with PRO
  • Mechanical royalties covered via The MLC / HFA
  • SoundExchange profile active and verified
  • Neighboring rights coverage evaluated
  • International sub-publishing assessed

Agreement Hygiene

  • Split sheets signed by all co-writers
  • Sampling clearances filed where applicable
  • Publishing administration agreement in place
  • Royalty statements accessible and filed
